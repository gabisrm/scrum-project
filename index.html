WHAT IS A PANGOLIN? Pangolins are insect-eating mammals covered in tough, overlapping scales.

Habitat

Where do we live?

Pangolins can be found across Asia and sub-Saharan Africa in sandy areas of woodlands and savannas. Their homes, underground burrows, are usually near water sources.

Diet

Pangolins are insectivorous

Most of their diet consists of various species of ants and termites and may be supplemented by other insects, especially larvae. They are somewhat particular and tend to consume only one or two species of insects, even when many species are available to them. A pangolin can consume 140 to 200 grams (5 to 7 ounces) ofinsects per day.[29]Pangolins are an important regulator of termite populations in their natural habitats.[30]
